A grayscale image can be represented as a M x N matrix of pixels. To adapt our problem to ACO, we represent our image as a graph where pixels are nodes connected to adjacent pixels.

   This configuration is quite similar to TSP. However, TSP graph was fully connected while our image is represented as partially connected graph: an ant can only travel from one pixel to a direct neighbour.

   In TSP, a solution was evaluated according to its length (the shorter, the better). Here, we substitute the length by a heuristic information derived from the intensity variation (the higher the better) along a given path.
